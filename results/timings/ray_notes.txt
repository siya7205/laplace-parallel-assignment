Observations
1. Numerical consistency is preserved across all Ray block counts, since the center value and final max difference remain the same.
2. Ray performance becomes worse as the number of blocks increases on this laptop.
3. Halo exchange and coordination overhead dominate the runtime.
4. The compute time itself is smaller than the communication/coordination cost.
5. For this problem size and local hardware setup, Ray does not scale well.
6. Ray is much slower than MPI and OpenMP for this stencil-based iterative solver.
