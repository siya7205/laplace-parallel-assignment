Observations
1. Numerical correctness is preserved across all process counts, since the center value and final max difference remain consistent.
2. MPI Python performance improves strongly from 1 to 2 to 4 processes.
3. The best result here is at 4 processes: 11.516111 s.
4. Performance becomes worse at 8 processes compared to 4 processes, which suggests that communication and Python-level overhead start dominating.
5. The update loop is by far the largest hotspot in all cases.
6. Communication time is small at 1 process, modest at 2 and 4 processes, and becomes much larger at 8 processes.
7. Even though the update loop time continues to decrease at 8 processes, total runtime increases because synchronization and communication costs rise.
8. MPI Python is much slower than MPI C++, which indicates the effect of Python interpreter and NumPy-per-row overhead in the iterative loop.
