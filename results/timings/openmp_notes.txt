1. Numerical correctness is preserved across all thread counts, since the center value and final max difference match the serial C++ result.

3. Performance improves significantly from 1 to 2 to 4 threads.
4. The best result here is at 4 threads: 3.030280 s.
5. Performance becomes worse at 8 threads compared to 4 threads, suggesting diminishing returns and extra overhead.
6. The update loop benefits most from threading, since it is the largest hotspot.
7. The copy loop also speeds up, but less dramatically.
8. This suggests the solver is limited not only by computation but also by memory movement and parallel overhead.
